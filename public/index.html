Who We Are

Every child is God’s handiwork.

Project LIKHA is a scholarship and formation program rooted in faith and service, dedicated to nurturing the academic and spiritual growth of underprivileged yet promising youth within the church community.

We believe that every child is God's handiwork — and that with the right support, they can become servant-leaders who transform their families, their church, and their communities.

“For we are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do."” — Ephesians 2:10
Mission

To provide holistic support to underprivileged yet academically promising youth.

Vision

A transformed generation of educated, faith-driven servant-leaders who break the cycle of poverty, live out Christian values, and actively contribute to the growth of the church and society.

What We Do

Beyond financial aid, we walk with scholars.

Project LIKHA is designed as a holistic support system for students who need both opportunity and guidance.

01

Scholarship Assistance

Financial assistance for tuition and school-related expenses, awarded based on financial need, academic potential, and church involvement.

02

Spiritual Mentorship & Discipleship

Regular mentoring through assigned Life Group Leaders, Bible studies, character formation, and discipleship activities.

03

Academic Support

Tutoring, study support, career guidance, and practical learning workshops.

04

Life Skills Development

Training in communication, time management, financial literacy, and personal responsibility.

05

Community Service & Ministry

Required participation in church-led service projects such as Sunday school teaching, youth outreach, tutoring, and community missions.

06

Pastoral Care

Semi-annual reviews of academic progress, spiritual growth, and community involvement, with counseling and intervention when needed.

06

Sustainable Donor Partnership

Church-based scholarship fund supported by donors, partner organizations, local businesses, fundraising events, and the Adopt-a-Scholar program.

Who We Serve

Our Beneficiaries

We serve underprivileged children and youth from economically disadvantaged families within partner communities, including Area 2 of Brgy. Bagong Silangan and students from Silang, Cavite.

Academic Promise

Students who demonstrate strong academic potential and a genuine commitment to learning, regardless of the financial barriers they face.

Church Participation

Youth who are committed to personal growth and active involvement in church life, reflecting a heart for faith and community.

Verified Need

Families screened through home visits and barangay certification to ensure support reaches those who need it most.

Family Impact

Families of scholars who receive spiritual and social support, and the broader church community which gains future leaders and volunteers.
